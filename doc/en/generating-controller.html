Generating a Controller

Now that you have already created your model, simply rerun the command below to start generating the next layer of your application!

php ci first_project

Shortly after running the above command, must be shown the layers that can be generated.

The CrudIgniter displays the layers that can be generated, after the choice of controller, are suggested options based on your database.

When you're generating a controller, you can add validation on fields that will be shown, for this the CrudIgniter displays all types of validations available in CodeIgniter, and you can also create your own type, but this will be talked in a next step.

IMPORTANT: The CrudIgniter offers several ways of choosing how to validate your field, you can simply choose the desired number, choose the number 1 that ignores the field and adds no validation rule, concatenate options such as choosing the option 3,11 which means that you want to add the two rules to that field, and also choose the number 2 to enter your own rule, but in this case you should follow the CodeIgniter available options, such as the following example.

required|max_length[10]|min_length[2]

Upon execution, you will have a new file created in the folder application/controllers of your project.
